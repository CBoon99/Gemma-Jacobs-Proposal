Calibre you can put in front of a CEO — or a parent at the school gate.

Buyers do not hire “bouncers”; they hire licensed professionals who represent their brand, protect their licence to operate, and de-escalate before force is ever considered. This is the standard we sell.

Licence & vetting

Every deployment uses officers holding the correct SIA licence for the role — Door Supervision, Security Guarding or other licences as the site demands — with right-to-work checks and recruitment screening that meet insurance and client expectations.

IDs are checked on shift; no licence, no site. That is the first line of trust with schools, retailers, faith buildings and public-facing brands.

Experience & presence

We recruit for communication, judgement and bearing. Many of our people bring former military, policing or emergency-services backgrounds — used for discipline and resilience, not intimidation: calm presence, clear radios, confident customer service.

Uniform, grooming and briefing standards match what corporate security, education and retail audits expect. Supervisors hold the line on post orders, incident logs and handover — so clients see consistency, not chaos.

Evidence-led context (Bury & Greater Manchester).

Recorded crime — borough lens

The Bury public-health snapshot reports recorded crime in Bury at about 96 offences per 1,000 people in the twelve months to Q4 2024, down from about 106 per 1,000 in the prior annual window — still mid-pack versus similar areas. Use the chart as a conversation starter, then drill into Police.uk street-level maps for specific postcodes.

Greater Manchester Police has had periods of incomplete public dashboards; always cross-check any postcode story with Police.uk and local partnership papers.

Antisemitism & hate crime — careful facts

Greater Manchester hosts one of the largest Jewish communities outside London. After October 2023, both community-recorded antisemitic incidents (CST) and police-recorded religious hate crime (Home Office / ONS) showed sharp increases nationally and in several city regions. CST’s annual antisemitic incident reports are the standard community dataset; Home Office bulletins give police-recorded hate crime trends.

Framing for bosses: professional guarding and community security planning are part of a wider resilience picture alongside statutory agencies — not a replacement for policing, and not a promise of grant funding.
